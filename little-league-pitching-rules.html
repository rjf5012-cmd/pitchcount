Little League Style Pitching Rules Overview

Many youth baseball leagues use pitching rules that are inspired by, or closely modeled after, official Little League Baseball regulations. While each organization can set its own policies, the overall goals are the same: limit overuse, encourage rest, and protect young arms for the long term.

Typical daily pitch limits by age

Common Little League style pitching rules often include age-based daily limits such as:

League age Maximum pitches per day
7–850 pitches
9–1075 pitches
11–1285 pitches
13–1695 pitches

Your league may tweak these numbers, but the idea is always the same: younger players get lower limits, and older players can safely handle slightly more volume.

Rest day requirements

Little League style rules also include mandatory rest periods based on pitch count. For example, a pitcher who throws a high number of pitches must rest several full days before pitching again. This reduces cumulative stress on the shoulder and elbow.

Other common pitching restrictions

  • Limits on pitching and catching in the same game.
  • Rules about finishing the current batter once a pitch limit is reached.
  • Restrictions on multiple pitching appearances in the same day or event.
