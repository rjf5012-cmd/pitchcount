Youth Baseball Pitching Rest Day Chart

Use this youth baseball rest day chart to see how many days of rest a pitcher should get after an outing, based on the number of pitches thrown.

This chart is based on common Little League–style guidelines for players age 14 and under. Your league’s official rules may differ slightly, so always check your local rulebook.

Pitches thrown in a day Required days of rest
1–20 pitches 0 days (eligible to pitch the next day)
21–35 pitches 1 day of rest
36–50 pitches 2 days of rest
51–65 pitches 3 days of rest
66+ pitches 4 days of rest

For players ages 15–16, many leagues use slightly higher pitch thresholds, but still require up to four full days of rest after high-pitch outings. When in doubt, choose more rest rather than less.

  • Track pitches for the entire day, including tournament doubleheaders.
  • Do not allow pitchers to “chase velocity” when they show signs of fatigue.
  • Keep open communication between coaches and parents about total pitching workload.

This chart is a general guideline and is not medical advice. If a player reports pain, fatigue, or discomfort, shut them down and consult a qualified professional before they pitch again.
